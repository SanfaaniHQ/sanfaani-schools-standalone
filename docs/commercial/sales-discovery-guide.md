# Sales Discovery Guide

Use this guide to understand the school before recommending a package. It helps sales and implementation teams ask better questions without promising unfinished automation.

## School Owner Or Proprietor

Likely concerns:

- Professional school image.
- Operational control.
- Cost predictability.
- Support availability.
- Fear of data loss or failed updates.

Useful questions:

- Do you want a dedicated private installation or a hosted subscription conversation?
- Who will own hosting, backups, and support decisions?
- Which workflows are most painful today?
- What support response expectations do you need?
- Do you need branding polish or white-label terms?

## Principal Or Head Teacher

Likely concerns:

- Academic setup clarity.
- Attendance discipline.
- Result visibility.
- Teacher workflow adoption.
- Parent-facing confidence.

Useful questions:

- How do you manage sessions, terms, classes, and subjects today?
- Which reports do you need every week?
- How do teachers submit records or learning materials today?
- What should parents see or not see?

## Bursar Or Accountant

Likely concerns:

- Fee tracking confusion.
- Invoice and payment history.
- Outstanding balances.
- Manual reconciliation.
- Report confidence.

Useful questions:

- Do you need fee items, assignments, invoices, manual payments, and balances?
- Which finance reports matter most?
- Who is allowed to view finance data?
- Do you require payment gateway automation now, or can manual recording work first?

## ICT Or Admin Officer

Likely concerns:

- Hosting setup.
- User accounts and permissions.
- Backups and updates.
- Storage permissions.
- Support handoff.

Useful questions:

- Will this run on cPanel, VPS, local server, or managed hosting?
- Who can run commands or contact the host?
- How will backups be created and verified?
- Who handles updates, support tickets, and release notes?

## Admissions Officer

Likely concerns:

- Lost application records.
- Manual document tracking.
- Status follow-up.
- Public admission form readiness.

Useful questions:

- How are applications collected today?
- Do you need public form links from an existing website?
- Who reviews documents, interviews, payments, and decisions?
- Is website integration required now, or can Laravel handle admissions directly first?

## Academic Coordinator

Likely concerns:

- Learning material organization.
- CBT connection.
- Live class scheduling.
- Teacher assignment boundaries.

Useful questions:

- Which classes and subjects need LMS materials?
- Do you already use CBT exams or quizzes?
- Do live classes need manual meeting links or future provider automation?
- Do teachers need training before launch?

## Parent-Facing Communication Concerns

Likely concerns:

- Clear notices.
- Professional branding.
- Confidence in reports and payment communication.
- Avoiding confusing or duplicated messages.

Useful questions:

- Which notifications should be logged or sent?
- Is email enough now, or are SMS/WhatsApp provider integrations a future requirement?
- Which parent-facing wording needs approval before launch?

## Common Pain Points To Listen For

- Scattered student records.
- Fee tracking confusion.
- Admission tracking issues.
- Manual attendance.
- Report visibility gaps.
- Branding and professionalism concerns.
- Learning and CBT digitization needs.
- Backup/update fear.
- Lack of support documentation.

## Qualification Red Flags

- Buyer requires full offline portal behavior before purchase.
- Buyer requires payment gateway automation as the main value.
- Buyer requires automated Google Meet, Zoom, or Microsoft Teams room creation.
- Buyer requires a Next.js public school website now.
- Buyer requires legal compliance guarantees without separate legal review.
- Buyer expects unlimited customization under a fixed package.
